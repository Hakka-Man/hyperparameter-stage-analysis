New York Stock Exchange

NYSE is owned by an American holding company named Intercontinental Exchanges and was established on May 17, 1792. The headquarter is located at the corner of Broad and Wall Street in New York City.

History of New York Stock Exchange

On May 28, 1972, at 68 wall street stockbrokers from New York City signed the Buttonwood agreement with NYSE.  The NYSE had initiated five securities, which included three government bonds and two bank stocks. The company name initially was the New York Stocks and Exchange board then is replaced with New York Stock Exchange in the year 1863.

From 1861 to 1865, after the Civil War, the exchange provided the capital for the accelerating industrialization of the US.

Requirements for corporate companies on NYSE

There are some minimum requirements for companies to meet the listing criteria for its place is NYSE. To listing in it, it requires them to already have 1.1 million publicly traded shares outstanding with a collective market value of at least $100 million.

How does NYSE work

In the past NYSE was only traded on the physical trading floor with the outcry system. Now, all the tradings are done on the physical trading floor and also electronically, while physically traders still set pricing and deal in institutional trading high volume.

After the pandemic, physical trading is dismissed in the US, and all the tradings are handled electronically through computers. Till now, physical trading has not come back to the NYSE.
